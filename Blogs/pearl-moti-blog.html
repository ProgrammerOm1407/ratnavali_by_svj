Precious Care

Caring for Pearl

Gentle Monthly Cleansing

Pearl is exceptionally delicate and absorbent — never use commercial jewelry cleaners, ultrasonic devices, or steam cleaners as they will permanently damage the nacre (mother-of-pearl coating). Instead, once per month, use only lukewarm distilled water and a soft, lint-free microfiber cloth. Wipe gently in circular motions and dry immediately. The safest approach: after each wear, simply wipe the pearl with a clean, dry, soft cloth to remove body oils and perspiration.

Never soak Pearl in water. Never use ultrasonic or steam cleaning. Risk of permanent nacre destruction.

Milk or Honey Bath (Traditional)

In Vedic tradition, Pearl may be gently cleansed using organic whole milk or raw honey. Soak a soft cloth in milk or honey, wipe the pearl gently, and immediately rinse with barely damp distilled water, then dry. This traditional method is considered auspicious — milk is associated with lunar energy and gentleness, while honey is believed to restore the pearl's natural luminosity. Perform this cleansing on Mondays during Shukla Paksha for maximum auspiciousness.

Traditional method only. Use organic, unpasteurized milk or raw honey. Rinse immediately with distilled water.

What to Never Use

Absolutely avoid: chlorine, saltwater, perfume, cosmetics, lotions, hairspray, alcohol, acetone, acidic cleaners, abrasive cloths, and any commercial jewelry cleaning solutions. Pearl's nacre is an organic calcium carbonate compound — acids and harsh chemicals dissolve it irreversibly. All these substances will dull the lustre, weaken the structure, and shorten the pearl's lifespan significantly.

Severity cannot be overstated: one exposure to harsh chemicals can permanently degrade a pearl.

Dedicated Storage Pouch

Always store Pearl in a separate, dedicated silk or white velvet pouch — never combined with other gemstones, metals, or jewelry. Pearl is softer than diamonds and sapphires; storing it alongside harder stones will inevitably result in scratches and surface degradation. The pouch should be soft-lined, breathable, and kept in a cool, dry location away from direct sunlight, heat sources, and extreme temperature fluctuations.

Separate storage is non-negotiable. Pearl scratches easily — protect it from friction with harder materials.

Climate & Environmental Conditions

Pearl thrives in moderate humidity (45–55%) and stable temperature. Extremely dry air will cause the pearl to shrink, crack, and become brittle. Excessive moisture promotes nacre deterioration and mold growth. Avoid storing Pearl in safety deposit boxes (too dry) or bathrooms (too humid). Ideal storage is a drawer in a naturally climate-controlled bedroom — cool, dark, and moderately humid.

Humidity and temperature stability are critical. Pearl is a living organic gem that requires environmental respect.

A Damaged or Dulled Pearl

If a Pearl becomes scratched, cracked, or develops a dull, chalky appearance, it signals significant lunar imbalance within the wearer's subtle body. A damaged or dulled pearl should be discontinued immediately and replaced with a fresh, natural certified pearl. In Vedic tradition, a worn or damaged pearl is believed to have absorbed negative planetary influences or emotional disturbances from the wearer. Do not continue wearing it for astrological purposes. Consult a Jyotishi for guidance on rituals before replacement. Perform a Moon worship (Chandra Puja) with fresh flowers, milk offerings, and the Chandra mantra to re-establish lunar harmony.

A damaged Pearl indicates significant lunar imbalance — discontinue use and perform Chandra Puja before replacement.

Removing Pearl Permanently

If a Jyotishi advises discontinuing the pearl, or if depression, emotional overwhelm, or sleep disturbances persist after wearing, removal must follow protocol. Remove the pearl on a Monday at sunrise. Immerse it briefly in a flowing river or natural water source — returning the Moon's energy to the ocean. Alternatively, bury it in white earth near a temple of Shiva or a sacred water source. Never casually discard or gift a worn pearl; the wearer's lunar imprint is considered strongly embedded in a worn stone.

Ritual removal respects the pearl's absorbed energies. Follow proper protocol to avoid karmic complications.

Cat's Eye Lehsunia

The Mystical Stone of Intuition, Protection & Karmic Liberation

Cat's Eye is the most enigmatic and powerful of all Vedic gemstones — prized for the extraordinary chatoyant band of light that glides across its surface like the slit pupil of a watching cat. In Vedic astrology, Cat's Eye resonates with Ketu, the shadow planet governing past karma, spiritual liberation, sudden events, and the hidden forces that shape destiny. Wear this luminous gem to awaken intuition, dissolve karmic obstacles, and align with the mysterious workings of fate.

Important: Cat's Eye is among the most powerful Vedic stones and must only be worn after thorough astrological consultation. Inauspicious Ketu placements can cause sudden reversals. Wear only certified natural chrysoberyl Cat's Eye with GIA/IGI certification.

Precious Care

Caring for Cat's Eye

Gentle Monthly Cleansing

Chrysoberyl Cat's Eye is a hard and relatively durable stone (Mohs 8.5), but its chatoyant silky inclusions can be masked by oils and surface residue. Clean monthly using lukewarm clean water and a soft bristle brush or lint-free microfiber cloth. Work gently around the cabochon surface and rinse under running water, patting dry immediately with a soft cloth. Avoid leaving the stone wet, as moisture can affect the setting metal over time. After each wear, wipe down the stone with a clean dry cloth to remove body oils and maintain the clarity of the chatoyancy band.

Never use ultrasonic or steam cleaners on Cat's Eye — vibrations can stress inclusions that create the chatoyant effect.

Energetic Cleansing (Traditional)

In Vedic tradition, Cat's Eye is cleansed of absorbed energies through ritual, not water alone. Place the stone in raw rice overnight — rice is believed to absorb negative energies accumulated in the stone. Alternatively, bury it briefly in clean dry earth and retrieve after a few hours. Expose the stone to incense smoke (particularly dhoop or sandalwood) while reciting the Ketu mantra. Perform energetic cleansing monthly on a Thursday or Saturday evening during the dark phase (Krishna Paksha) of the Moon for maximum effect.

Energetic cleansing is as important as physical cleaning for astrological gemstones. Ketu absorbs and transmutes significant energy.

What to Never Use

Avoid chlorine, bleach, strong acids, abrasive cloths, ultrasonic cleaners, steam, and commercial jewellery dips. While chrysoberyl is hard, aggressive chemical exposure can dull the surface lustre, affect the rutile needle inclusions responsible for chatoyancy, and damage the gold setting. Perfumes, hairsprays, and cosmetics should always be applied before putting on the ring. Sweat and body chemistry can also dull the stone over time — regular wiping after wear prevents build-up.

Protecting the chatoyancy band is paramount — it is the very soul of the stone.

Dedicated Storage Pouch

Store Cat's Eye in a separate, dedicated silk or velvet pouch — ideally dark-coloured (black, brown, or deep gold) in keeping with Ketu's energetic nature. Do not store alongside softer gemstones, as chrysoberyl at Mohs 8.5 will scratch emerald, pearl, coral, and other softer stones. Equally, diamonds and corundum can abrade the surface of Cat's Eye if jostled together. Individual compartmented jewellery boxes or separate pouches are the safest option. Keep in a cool, dry location away from direct sunlight.

Individual storage is non-negotiable for an astrological gemstone of this significance.

Climate & Environmental Conditions

Cat's Eye is a stable stone that tolerates a range of environmental conditions better than organic gems like Pearl or Coral. However, avoid prolonged exposure to direct strong sunlight, which may bleach or alter the body colour of some specimens over years. Extreme temperature fluctuations are not advisable for the metal setting. Store in a stable, moderate-temperature location — a bedroom drawer or jewellery chest is ideal. Remove during activities that may cause physical impact or chemical exposure.

Cat's Eye is robust but deserves respectful stewardship as a powerful planetary stone.

A Damaged or Dulled Cat's Eye

If a Cat's Eye becomes scratched, chips, or the chatoyancy band loses its sharpness and brilliance, it is a significant sign in Vedic tradition. A damaged stone is believed to have absorbed a heavy karmic burden or deflected a severe misfortune on the wearer's behalf — it has "done its duty." Such a stone should be discontinued immediately and not worn for astrological purposes. Do not attempt to re-polish a Cat's Eye that has weakened chatoyancy, as the needle inclusions creating the effect may be permanently disrupted. Consult a Jyotishi before replacement and perform Ketu worship rituals to re-establish planetary harmony.

A damaged Cat's Eye signals significant Ketu activity — discontinue use and perform Ketu Puja before replacement.

Removing Cat's Eye Permanently

If a Jyotishi advises discontinuing Cat's Eye, or if anxiety, irrational fears, sudden losses, or health disturbances persist after wearing, removal must follow protocol. Remove the stone on a Thursday or Saturday at dusk. Immerse it briefly in flowing water — returning Ketu's energy to the earth. Alternatively, bury it in dark earth near a Ketu shrine, Ganesha temple, or a sacred banyan or ashoka tree. Never casually discard or gift a worn Cat's Eye; the wearer's karmic imprint is considered deeply embedded in a worn stone, and improper disposal invites karmic complications.

Ritual removal respects the stone's absorbed karmic energies. Follow proper protocol to avoid complications.

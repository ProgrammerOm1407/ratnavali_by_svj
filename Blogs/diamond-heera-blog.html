Precious Care

Caring for Diamond

Regular Physical Cleaning

Diamond is the hardest natural substance on earth (Mohs 10) and is chemically inert — making it one of the more forgiving gems to clean physically. Soak the ring in a bowl of warm water with a few drops of mild dish soap for 20–30 minutes to loosen body oils and residue. Gently scrub around the facets and under the setting with a soft-bristle toothbrush, rinse under running water, and pat dry with a lint-free cloth. Perform this cleaning every two to three weeks for stones worn daily. Professional ultrasonic cleaning is safe for diamonds, but only with a secure, undamaged setting — always confirm with your jeweller first.

Body oils are the primary enemy of diamond's brilliance. Regular cleaning preserves the fire and scintillation that define a fine stone.

Energetic Cleansing (Traditional)

In Vedic tradition, Diamond is energetically cleansed and re-activated through specific rituals aligned with Venus. Place the stone in raw cow's milk overnight on a Thursday or Friday — milk is considered purifying and Venusian in nature. Remove, rinse with clean water, and dry thoroughly. Alternatively, expose the diamond to the light of the full moon overnight, placing it on a white cloth with fresh white flowers. Recite the Shukra mantra 108 times while holding the stone. This monthly renewal restores the gem's planetary resonance and strengthens the wearer's connection to Venus's grace.

Energetic renewal is as vital as physical care. A diamond in active astrological use accumulates planetary and personal energies continuously.

What to Never Use

While diamond's surface is extraordinarily hard, chlorine bleach and other harsh chemicals can damage the metal setting — platinum and gold are both vulnerable to repeated chemical exposure. Avoid chlorine pools, household bleach, acetone, and strong solvents. Hairspray and perfume leave residue that dulls surface brilliance. Never use abrasive cleaning powders or rough cloths on the setting metalwork. Though diamond cannot be scratched by other gems, its setting can be distorted by impact — avoid wearing during heavy physical activity, moving furniture, or contact sports.

The diamond itself is indestructible under normal conditions, but its setting and the stone's edges require thoughtful protection.

Dedicated Storage Pouch

Store Diamond in a separate, dedicated white or pale silk pouch, or in its own lined compartment in a jewellery box. Diamond at Mohs 10 is harder than every other gemstone — if stored carelessly alongside rubies, sapphires, emeralds, or any softer stone, it will scratch them. Conversely, two diamonds stored together can chip and abrade each other along their crystallographic planes. Individual storage is not optional — each diamond deserves its own space. For Vedic astrological use, many practitioners recommend white or ivory silk to honour Venus's association with purity, light, and refinement.

Individual storage is mandatory. Diamond will damage every other gem it contacts — including other diamonds.

Climate & Environmental Conditions

Diamond is chemically inert and the most thermally stable of all gemstones — it tolerates humidity, temperature variation, and most environmental conditions with ease. However, certain treated diamonds (HPHT-treated or fracture-filled stones) can be affected by heat and chemicals. This is why certification of a natural, untreated stone is essential for astrological use. Store in a clean, dry environment, away from loose chemicals or abrasive materials. The setting metal — platinum or gold — is more environmentally sensitive than the diamond itself, so stable, moderate conditions preserve the jewellery's overall integrity.

Certification of natural, untreated status is critical. Treated diamonds behave differently and may be unsuitable for astrological purposes.

A Chipped, Cracked, or Clouded Diamond

Though diamond is the hardest substance on earth, it is not immune to cleavage fracture — a sharp impact along the crystallographic grain can chip or split even a large, flawless stone. A chipped or internally fractured diamond should be discontinued immediately for astrological use. In Vedic tradition, a damaged diamond is regarded with utmost seriousness — it is believed to have intercepted a major blow of fate or absorbed significant negative Venusian energy on the wearer's behalf. A dull, oily-looking diamond that does not regain its fire after thorough cleaning may have undergone micro-fracturing. Consult a Jyotishi and a gemologist before replacing. Perform Shukra Puja with white flowers, kheer, and Venus mantra recitation before wearing a new stone.

A damaged diamond signals significant Shukra activity — discontinue use, consult a Jyotishi, and perform Shukra Puja before replacement.

Removing Diamond Permanently

If a Jyotishi advises discontinuing the diamond, or if excess indulgence, emotional attachment, relationship turbulence, or financial recklessness intensifies after wearing, removal must follow protocol. Remove the ring on a Friday at sunrise. Cleanse the stone in raw milk, then running water. Wrap the diamond in white silk and place it at the base of a tulsi or jasmine plant — both sacred to Venus — or offer it at a Shukra shrine or Lakshmi temple. Never casually discard or gift a worn diamond; the stone is deeply imbued with the wearer's Venusian imprint, and improper transfer can carry relational or financial karmic consequences.

Ritual removal respects the diamond's absorbed Venus energies. Follow proper protocol to close the planetary connection honourably.

About the Stone

What is Yellow Sapphire?

Yellow Sapphire, known as Pukhraj in Sanskrit, is one of the most auspicious gemstones in Vedic astrology. A variety of corundum coloured by trace amounts of iron, it ranks 9 on the Mohs hardness scale — second only to diamond.

Its golden luminosity ranges from pale lemon to deep amber. Ancient texts describe it as a vessel of Jupiter's divine energy, capable of channelling wisdom, abundance, and spiritual clarity to those who wear it.

Used in sacred contexts across India, Sri Lanka, and Southeast Asia for millennia, Pukhraj remains among the most revered planetary gemstones in Jyotish (Vedic astrology) tradition.

Varieties

Types of Pukhraj

Each origin produces a distinctly different character of stone. Geographic origin is a primary factor in a Yellow Sapphire's value and astrological potency.

Ceylon

Light to medium lemon-yellow with exceptional clarity and brilliance. Sri Lanka's finest; considered the most premium origin globally.

Burma

Rich golden yellow with vibrant saturation and distinctive natural silk inclusions. Highly prized for its depth of colour.

Madagascar

Deep canary-yellow with excellent luster. Offers good clarity at competitive value; increasingly important source for quality specimens.

Thailand

Medium yellow with good transparency and fine brilliance. Often heat-treated to enhance colour; widely available in various sizes.

Gemology

How to Identify a Genuine Stone

01

Hardness Test

With a Mohs hardness of 9, Yellow Sapphire will not be scratched by a steel file (hardness 6.5) or quartz (7). Glass scratches easily at 5.5 — a genuine stone will resist this. Never attempt this on a finished piece without expert guidance.

02

Refractive Index

A refractometer is the gemologist's primary tool. Corundum reads between 1.762 and 1.770 with a birefringence of 0.008. Glass and synthetic impostors will read differently. Lab certification (GIA, IGI) remains the gold standard.

03

Inclusions Under Loupe

Natural sapphires often contain characteristic rutile needle inclusions ("silk"), fingerprint inclusions, or growth zoning. A completely flawless stone at low price is a red flag. Examine under 10× magnification with a trained eye.

Maintenance

Complete Care Guide

Yellow Sapphire ranks 9 on the Mohs scale — second only to diamond — making it one of the most durable gemstones. Yet mindful care preserves both its physical brilliance and, in traditional belief, its energetic potency for decades.

Morning Handling

Always put on your Pukhraj ring after applying moisturiser, sunscreen, or perfume — never before. Chemical residue from these products can build up on the stone's surface over time, creating a dull film that reduces its natural brilliance. A few seconds of patience each morning will significantly extend the life of your stone's lustre.

Tip: Keep the ring in a small dish near your dressing table as a reminder.

Quick Wipe After Wearing

Before setting the ring down each evening, gently wipe the stone and setting with a dry, soft microfibre cloth. Skin oils, sweat, and fine dust accumulate daily — removing them immediately prevents build-up in crevices around the prongs and beneath the stone, which is especially important for open-back settings where the stone touches the skin.

Remove During Physical Activity

Always remove the ring before gym sessions, sports, gardening, cooking, or any manual work. Even at Mohs 9, a sharp impact at the right angle can chip or fracture corundum — particularly at girdle edges. The gold setting is also vulnerable to bending or deforming under force, which can loosen the prongs holding the stone.

Traditional belief: physical impact is also said to disrupt the stone's energetic alignment.

Home Soap Wash (Fortnightly)

Fill a small bowl with warm — never hot — water and add two drops of mild, pH-neutral dish soap. Soak the ring for 10–15 minutes to loosen oils and debris. Using a soft-bristled toothbrush (baby or cosmetic grade), gently scrub beneath the stone, around the prongs, and along the band. Rinse thoroughly under running lukewarm water — plug the drain first — and pat completely dry with a lint-free cloth. Allow to air-dry for 30 minutes before storing or wearing.

Frequency: Every two weeks for daily-wear rings; monthly for occasional wear.

Ultrasonic Cleaners

Ultrasonic cleaning is generally safe for untreated natural Yellow Sapphires, as corundum handles vibration well. However, you must never use ultrasonic cleaning if your stone has been fracture-filled, oiled, or has significant internal fractures — the vibrations can propagate existing cracks and cause the stone to shatter. When in doubt, use the soap-and-brush method. Always ask your gemologist about treatments when purchasing.

Never use ultrasonic cleaners on heat-treated stones with significant inclusions.

Professional Steam Cleaning

A professional jeweller's steam cleaner can reach into intricate settings that a brush cannot. This is safe for clean, untreated Yellow Sapphires and effectively removes stubborn grease build-up. Schedule this alongside your annual professional inspection. Steam at high pressure should be avoided for stones with surface-reaching fractures, as rapid thermal change can stress the stone.

What to Avoid Absolutely

Never use bleach, chlorine, ammonia-based glass cleaners, toothpaste, baking soda paste, or abrasive cloths on your Pukhraj. While the stone itself is scratch-resistant, these chemicals corrode gold settings, weaken solder joints, and can discolour metal over time. Similarly, avoid hand sanitisers — their high alcohol and hydrogen peroxide content is damaging to both metal and any adhesive used in the setting.

Pure warm water and mild soap is always the safest option.

Individual Compartment Storage

Store your Yellow Sapphire ring in a fabric-lined box with individual compartments or in its own soft pouch — never loose in a drawer with other jewellery. Despite being a 9 on the Mohs scale, sapphires can scratch softer stones like emeralds, pearls, or opals, and can in turn be scratched by diamonds (10). Isolation prevents surface abrasion that gradually dulls the stone's surface polish.

Velvet or silk pouches are ideal; avoid plastic bags that trap moisture.

Temperature & Light Conditions

Store in a cool, dry location away from direct sunlight, extreme heat, or damp conditions. Prolonged UV exposure can, over years, cause subtle colour shift in iron-coloured sapphires. Humidity can tarnish gold settings and, in extreme cases, promote corrosion at solder points. A bedroom drawer or jewellery safe — away from bathroom steam — is ideal.

Travel Storage

When travelling, use a dedicated travel jewellery roll or hard case with a lock. Place the ring in its own padded slot. Never pack gemstone jewellery loose in a suitcase where it can tumble against other objects. If carrying on, keep fine jewellery in your cabin bag — checked luggage is subject to rough handling and temperature extremes in cargo holds.

Airport X-ray scanners do not harm gemstones or gold.

☀️ Summer

Perspiration increases in heat — wipe the stone more frequently. Remove before swimming in the sea (salt water) or pools (chlorine). Sunscreen application is high; always put the ring on last. Store away from windowsill heat.

🌧️ Monsoon

High humidity can tarnish gold settings faster. Ensure the ring is completely dry before storing. Check prongs more frequently, as moisture can loosen microscopic solder joints over time. Avoid silica gel pouches directly touching the stone.

🍂 Autumn / Post-Monsoon

An excellent time for professional cleaning and inspection after the humid season. Dust the jewellery box and replace any silica sachets. Check that settings haven't shifted from seasonal expansion and contraction of the metal band.

❄️ Winter

Cold can cause fingers to shrink, making rings more prone to slipping off. Be extra mindful outdoors. Dry indoor heat from heaters can crack older gold settings. If the band feels looser than usual, visit a jeweller for resizing rather than forcing it.

Annual Inspection Rhythm

Schedule a professional jeweller inspection every 12 months, ideally at the start of summer before heavy wear. The jeweller will check prong tightness, inspect for micro-cracks in the stone, re-polish the setting if needed, and advise on any developing concerns before they become costly problems.

Monthly Panchamrit Cleansing

On the first Thursday of each month (or on any Shukla Paksha Thursday), place the ring in a small copper or silver bowl. Pour over it, in sequence: raw cow's milk, pure honey, ghee, curd (yoghurt), and Gangajal or clean water — the five sacred substances of Panchamrit. Let it rest for 15 minutes, then rinse with clean water and dry gently. Recite the Guru Beej Mantra — "Om Gram Greem Graum Sah Gurave Namah" — 108 times while the stone rests in the mixture.

This is an energetic practice; the stone does not require this for physical cleanliness.

Reinstallation After Extended Absence

If the ring has been set aside for more than 40 days — due to travel, illness, or ritual impurity — it should be re-consecrated before wearing again. Perform the Panchamrit cleansing, then hold the ring in folded hands facing east at sunrise on a Thursday. Visualise golden Jupiter light entering the stone. Recite "Om Brihaspetaye Namah" 108 times before placing it back on the index finger of the right hand.

If the Stone is Damaged or Lost

A cracked, chipped, or lost Yellow Sapphire is considered highly inauspicious in Vedic tradition, often interpreted as the stone having absorbed a significant negative event on behalf of the wearer. Do not continue wearing a cracked stone. Consult a qualified Jyotishi for guidance on the appropriate ritual response, and have the stone replaced with a new, certified natural Pukhraj re-consecrated through a proper installation ceremony.

Never replace with a glass or synthetic substitute without your astrologer's knowledge.

Passing the Stone On

If you wish to pass a Pukhraj to a family member — a common practice in Indian households — the stone should first be energetically cleared. Place it in a bowl of raw cow's milk for 24 hours, then leave it under morning sunlight (not harsh midday sun) for 2–3 hours on a Thursday. The new wearer should then perform a fresh consecration with their Jyotishi to tune the stone to their own Jupiter placement.

About the Stone

What is Ruby?

Ruby, known as Manik in Sanskrit, is the gemstone of the Sun in Vedic astrology. A variety of corundum coloured by chromium, its hues range from pale pinkish-red to the legendary "Pigeon's Blood" red of Burma — the most coveted and valuable ruby colour in the world.

Ranked 9 on the Mohs hardness scale, Manik is among the hardest of all gemstones. Its extraordinary lustre, intense red fire, and ability to fluoresce under ultraviolet light give it a living, inner glow that has captivated kings, emperors, and collectors across civilisations — from Mughal courts to medieval European crowns.

In Jyotish (Vedic astrology), Ruby is the gemstone of Surya — the Sun — the planet governing the soul, authority, government, health, and one's father. A strong Sun brings confidence, vitality, and recognition; a weak Sun invites self-doubt, poor health, and struggles with authority.

The Sun's Influence

The Dual Nature of Manik

Unlike softer planetary stones, Ruby is the direct representative of the Sun — the most fundamental force in a horoscope. Its effects on the wearer can be profound and immediate, depending entirely on the Sun's placement and strength in the birth chart.

When Beneficial

When the Sun is strong or the ascendant is Leo (Simha), Ruby can bring a surge in self-confidence, recognition from authority figures, improved career prospects, vitality, and the natural charisma of a born leader. It is often felt as warmth and clarity within days of wearing.

When Inauspicious

If the Sun rules malefic houses or is debilitated in the chart, wearing Ruby may amplify ego conflicts, headaches, eye strain, blood pressure issues, and strained relationships with one's father or authority figures. The fire of the Sun, misdirected, burns rather than illuminates.

Observing the Stone

Unlike Neelam, Ruby traditionally does not require a formal 3-day trial period. However, sensitivity to any new planetary stone is wise. Observe your energy levels, mood, and relationships in the days following first wear. Heightened confidence is expected; aggression or fever is a warning sign.

Vitality & Health

Fame & Recognition

Career & Government

Varieties

Origins of Manik

Geographic origin defines the colour character, fluorescence, and astrological grading of Ruby. Burmese (Myanmar) rubies with the legendary Pigeon's Blood colour command the highest prices of any coloured gemstone on earth.

Burma

The legendary Pigeon's Blood red — a vivid, pure red with a slight blue overtone and intense natural fluorescence. Mogok valley rubies are the world's most prized. Unheated Burmese Manik commands extraordinary value and astrological potency.

Ceylon

Sri Lankan rubies tend toward pinkish-red, with excellent transparency and brilliance. Considered highly auspicious for astrological use. Unheated Ceylon Manik is among the most accessible premium options for Vedic purposes.

Mozambique

A relatively recent source producing vivid, saturated rubies that rival Burmese material in colour intensity. Mozambique rubies often have excellent clarity at competitive prices and are gaining acceptance among astrological gemologists.

Thailand

Thai rubies tend toward darker, brownish-red tones with higher iron content. Heavily treated material from Thailand dominates the commercial market. Natural, unheated Thai rubies are rare; careful certification is essential for astrological use.

Gemology

How to Identify a Genuine Stone

01

Fluorescence Test

Natural rubies fluoresce strongly red under ultraviolet (UV) light due to chromium — this is one of the most distinguishing characteristics of a genuine ruby. Synthetic rubies also fluoresce, but glass imitations and red garnets do not. Burmese rubies show the strongest fluorescence of all origins.

02

Heat Treatment Detection

Over 95% of commercial rubies are heat-treated to dissolve silk inclusions and improve colour. For Vedic astrological use, unheated rubies are strongly preferred. Only a certified gemological laboratory — GIA, GRS, or Gübelin — can reliably confirm the absence of heat treatment through microscopic examination and spectroscopy.

03

Natural Inclusions

Under 10× magnification, natural rubies show characteristic rutile silk needles, fingerprint inclusions, and growth zoning. Star rubies display a six-rayed asterism from fine intersecting silk. Completely clean stones at low prices are almost certainly synthetic. Lead-glass filled rubies — a common fraud — show trapped gas bubbles and bluish flash.

Maintenance

Complete Care Guide

Ruby is one of the hardest and most durable gemstones on earth, but specific care — both physical and energetic — is essential to preserve its brilliance and, in Vedic tradition, its potency as the solar stone. The Sun's gem deserves the reverence due to royalty.

Morning Protocol

Apply all perfume, moisturiser, sunscreen, and hand creams before placing your Manik ring on your finger. Chemical residues from these products accumulate on the stone's surface, dulling its signature fire and red brilliance. As the Sun's stone, Ruby thrives when honoured with conscious daily attention — a small ritual that aligns with the solar energy it carries.

Tradition: Ideally wear your Manik ring after morning prayers and after offering water to the Sun (Surya Arghya) for maximum alignment.

Evening Wipe Routine

Before storing each evening, wipe the ring gently with a soft, dry microfibre cloth. Remove accumulated skin oils, sweat, and fine dust — especially from beneath the stone where the open-back setting allows debris to collect. In Vedic tradition, this contact point between gem and skin is where the Sun's energy enters the body; keeping it clear ensures unobstructed energy transfer.

Removal During Activities

Remove your Manik ring before the gym, swimming, cooking, gardening, and chemical-contact cleaning. Although corundum is extremely hard, sharp lateral impacts can chip the girdle edges, and gold settings can bend under heavy force, loosening prongs and risking loss of the stone. A lost or damaged Manik requires immediate attention from a Jyotishi.

Never sleep wearing the ring — prongs can deform against hard surfaces overnight.

Fortnightly Soap Wash

Fill a small bowl with lukewarm water and two drops of mild, pH-neutral dish soap. Soak the ring for 10–15 minutes. Use a soft-bristled brush — a baby toothbrush works perfectly — to clean gently around the prongs, beneath the stone, and along the band. Rinse under lukewarm running water with the drain plugged. Dry completely with a lint-free cloth and air for 30 minutes before storing. Sunday mornings are the auspicious choice for this cleaning, aligned with the Sun's day.

Frequency: Fortnightly for daily wear; monthly for occasional wear.

Ultrasonic Cleaning

Safe only for clean, unheated, untreated natural rubies with no significant fractures. Lead-glass filled rubies — extremely common in the market — will be shattered immediately by ultrasonic vibration. Always verify your stone's treatment status via your GIA or GRS laboratory certificate before attempting any ultrasonic cleaning. When uncertain, warm soap and a brush is always safe.

Never use ultrasonic cleaning on a fracture-filled or clarity-enhanced ruby — it causes instant, irreversible damage.

What to Strictly Avoid

Never use bleach, chlorine, ammonia, baking soda, toothpaste, or abrasive glass cleaners on your Manik. Gold settings react poorly to chlorine; prongs can weaken over time with repeated exposure. Hand sanitisers with high alcohol content damage the finish of gold settings and can attack fracture-fill treatments. Use only mild soap and water, always lukewarm and never boiling.

Annual Professional Service

Schedule a professional jeweller inspection every 12 months. The jeweller will check prong tightness, examine the stone for developing fractures, re-polish the gold setting, check solder joints, and reach areas inaccessible to a home brush. Annual servicing is particularly important for gold settings as they can develop hairline cracks at prong bases and solder points with daily wear.

Individual Storage

Store your Manik ring in its own dedicated pouch — red or gold-coloured velvet is traditional and appropriate — or in a separate compartment of a jewellery box. Do not store with diamonds (which can scratch corundum surfaces at certain angles) or with Blue Sapphire — in Vedic tradition, the Sun and Saturn are planetary enemies and their stones should not cohabit the same space.

Tradition: Ruby should never be stored alongside Neelam (Blue Sapphire) or Hessonite (Gomed) — inimical planetary energies.

Environment & Conditions

Store in a cool, dry, and dark environment. Unlike some gemstones, Ruby is generally stable in light, but prolonged direct sunlight will fade certain fracture-fill treatments over years. Avoid bathroom storage — steam and humidity accelerate tarnishing of gold settings and can loosen older prong work. A dedicated locked jewellery box in a bedroom is ideal for both security and energetic sanctity.

Travel Precautions

When travelling with your Manik, always use a padded hard-case jewellery organiser in carry-on luggage only. Cargo hold conditions are not suitable for fine gemstone rings. X-ray machines do not harm corundum. When visiting temples where jewellery must be removed, carry a small dedicated red pouch rather than placing the ring loose in a bag or pocket where it may be dropped or forgotten.

☀️ Summer

Peak season for wearing Manik — the stone resonates with summer solar energy. However, increased perspiration means more frequent wiping is necessary. Remove before swimming in pools (chlorine) or the sea (salt). Sunscreen and sweat build-up on gold settings tarnishes the finish; clean more frequently in hot months.

🌧️ Monsoon

Highest risk season for tarnishing gold settings and loosening prongs due to constant moisture cycles. Always dry the ring completely before storing. Check monthly that the stone hasn't shifted in its setting after water exposure. Store with a small silica gel packet near (not touching) the ring to control box humidity.

🍂 Post-Monsoon

An ideal time for professional cleaning and inspection following the humid season. Have the jeweller check for any oxidation or tarnish build-up in crevices of the gold setting, re-polish as needed, and verify prong integrity after potential thermal cycles during the wet season. Consider re-consecration if the ring was stored for extended periods.

❄️ Winter

Cold causes fingers to contract — the ring may sit looser and risk slipping off. If it becomes notably loose, have it resized rather than wearing it on a different finger, which changes its astrological alignment. Dry indoor heating can stress older solder joints in gold settings over time. A light buff with a gold polishing cloth maintains lustre through winter.

The Sun's Weekly Honouring

Every Sunday, as the Sun's day, is considered the ideal time for a brief wipe-clean of your Manik and a moment of intentional acknowledgement of its significance. The traditional practice of offering water to the rising sun (Surya Arghya) while wearing the ring, or holding it toward the morning light for a few moments, is considered a powerful way to energise the stone weekly — aligning the solar gem with its source.

Sunday Panchamrit Cleansing

On the first Sunday of each month during Shukla Paksha, place the ring in a clean copper or gold bowl. Pour over it in sequence: raw cow's milk, pure honey, ghee, curd, and clean water — the five Panchamrit substances. Let it rest for 15 minutes, then rinse and dry gently. While the stone rests, recite the Surya Beej Mantra — "Om Hraam Hreem Hraum Sah Suryaya Namah" — 108 times with red flowers offered before a Sun image. This is an energetic ritual; physical soap cleaning is separate.

Copper or gold vessel is associated with Surya; both are appropriate for this ritual.

Re-consecration After Absence

If the ring has not been worn for more than 40 days — due to illness, travel, ritual impurity, or any other reason — it must be re-energised before resuming wear. Perform the Panchamrit cleansing on a Sunday morning. Hold the ring facing east toward the rising sun. Recite "Om Suryaya Namah" 108 times, visualising golden solar light filling the stone completely. Only then place it on the ring finger of the right hand. This re-establishes the solar connection that absence may have weakened.

If the Stone Cracks, Chips, or is Lost

A damaged or lost Manik is considered a significant event in Vedic tradition — interpreted as the stone having absorbed a major negative karmic or solar energy disturbance that would otherwise have befallen the wearer. Do not attempt to re-set a cracked stone for astrological use. Consult a Jyotishi immediately. Perform a Surya Shanti Puja and replace with a new certified, natural, unheated stone through a fresh consecration ceremony on a Sunday.

A cracked Manik is considered an important sign requiring ritual response — do not ignore or continue wearing it.

Removing Manik Permanently

If a Jyotishi advises discontinuing the stone, or if consistent adverse effects are observed, removal must follow protocol. Remove on a Sunday, ideally at sunrise. Immerse the ring briefly in flowing water or bury it in pure earth at the base of a pipal or banyan tree — traditional methods of returning solar energy to its source. Never casually discard or gift a Manik that has been worn; the wearer's solar imprint remains in the stone. A Jyotishi should advise on the appropriate closing ritual.
